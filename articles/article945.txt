<p>Yesterday, Jennifer Klein, Director of the White House Gender Policy Council, and Rohini Kosoglu, Domestic Policy Advisor to the Vice President, hosted a meeting with reproductive justice leaders from across the country. <br/>Reproductive justice leaders shared their concerns about access to reproductive health care on the ground, describing the range of challenges that patients currently face as states pass extreme laws restricting access to critical health care. Participants also highlighted the disproportionate impact that abortion restrictions have on underserved communities, including communities of color and low-income women.<br/><br/>On Thursday, the Vice President <a href="https://twitter.com/VP/status/1527351664432525317?s=20&amp;t=-WpEoNePRl1rtAw-uqMjQw">met</a> with doctors and nurses to discuss the impact that overturning <em>Roe v. Wade </em>would have on women’s health care. Later the day, White House Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre issued a <a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/briefing-room/statements-releases/2022/05/19/statement-from-press-secretary-karine-jean-pierre-on-the-oklahoma-abortion-bill/">statement</a> on the Oklahoma legislature’s latest bill, which is one of the most extreme efforts yet to undo fundamental reproductive rights.<br/><br/>Reproductive justice meeting participants included:</p>
