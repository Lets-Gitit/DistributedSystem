<p class="has-text-align-center">The White House</p>
<p>Thank you! It’s my pleasure to welcome all of you—so many good friends—to the White House!<br/><br/>And we are pleased to welcome the Taoiseach of Ireland, Micheál Martin, and his wife Mary, as well as the Irish Delegation, to Washington, D.C.<br/><br/>To love Joe Biden is to know Ireland. Picture me on our first few dates, dressed in bell bottoms and a peasant shirt. <br/><br/>I wore my hair down to my waist—and at the time, so did most of the men I dated. <br/><br/>And then there was this Senator, handsome and buttoned up in his suit and loafers—casually quoting Yeats.<br/><br/>It was the last thing I expected—but in the end, he won me over. <br/><br/>It doesn’t matter when Joe’s ancestors left—a piece of that island was passed down to each generation in memories, and stories, and family lore, held close like a precious gem—an emerald heart that shines like the light after a storm.<br/><br/>It’s a loyalty that knows “family” means forever.<br/><br/>A faith—tried and tested by famine and war.<br/><br/>An optimism brave enough to leave the life you know behind, and find what’s waiting on distant shores.<br/><br/>He’s not alone, of course. <br/><br/>Like so many generations of immigrants, Irish Americans brought the best of their home with them on wind and steam ships so many years ago. <br/><br/>Ridiculed and despised, they found ways to create new traditions, while never letting go of the legacies that made them, enriching our country along the way.<br/><br/>So it’s fitting that we honor that heritage by celebrating Saint Patrick, a man who brought faith to Ireland, enriching it, too. <br/><br/>There has always been a divine spark in the open arms of welcome. <br/><br/>Yes, I got to know Ireland through the eyes of Joe Biden. <br/><br/>On St. Patrick’s Day, all Americans, no matter their background, fall in love with it too.<br/><br/>We reach for a legacy buried deep inside of us—or gifted second-hand.<br/><br/>We celebrate a spirit that defies struggle.<br/><br/>We recognize the rich tapestry of our history—threads of clover green amid the red, white, and blue. <br/><br/>Happy St. Patrick’s Day. </p>
